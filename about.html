The Work That
Reconnects

About

Since 1978, The Work That Reconnects has inspired millions of people to listen to their hearts and work together for a better life on Earth, despite the general trend of worsening ecological conditions. To learn the basics of The Work That Reconnects and its unique approach, people participate in experiences of varying duration, from a few days to a few weeks. But, The Work That Reconnects extends far beyond these dedicated frameworks. The methods are widely used in schools, communities, organizations, and campaigns for the environment and human rights. The central purpose of this work is to bring people into relationship with the world around them, empowering them to act and take part in "The Great Turning".

Benefits

  • A safe container to express and feel your feelings in regard to what is happening in the world
  • Finding new tools and practices that can help you in your day-to-day life and in your relationships, communities, and work life
  • Gaining new perspectives about the cultureship that is needed in this world
  • Covering your inner resources and your skills for The Great Turning
  • Building a strong network and creating a community of like-minded people

Climate Circles

About

A Climate Circle is a safe and empathetic space where you can express your feelings regarding the climate and ecological crisis while exploring your inner responses and discovering your role in the Great Turning.

Benefits

  • As the danger of the climate crisis to our safety and health becomes more and more clear, we increasingly need to talk about how these changes are impacting us
  • Normalizing and validating your feelings, which may be taboo or difficult to express
  • Being part of a circle where you can feel seen and heard and with whom you can share similar values and enrich your perspectives and hope
  • The climate circle has a structure and acts as a container where you can explore your fear, eco-anxiety, anger, hopelessness, helplessness, grief, and depression while staying connected and grounded
  • You can discover somatic and mindfulness practices that help you maintain and enrich your resilience

Forest Bathing

About

Forest Bathing is the English translation of the Japanese term "shinrin yoku" (shinrin=forest and yoku=bath). It is the practice of experiencing natural spaces, especially forests, in a slow, purposeful manner. Forest Bathing was developed in the 1980s, and it has become an increasingly important part of Japanese preventive care and therapeutic healing, becoming an antidote to our unnatural, indoor lives. Participants engage all their senses, paying attention to what is happening inside them, outside them, and how these worlds meet and interact.

Benefits

  • Supports the Immune System
  • Reduce stress levels
  • Helps to maintain healthy blood pressure levels
  • Improves mood and alleviates anxiety
  • It enhances brain function and increases creativity
  • Increase your natural killer cells
  • Faster recovery from illness
  • Cardiovascular and metabolic improvements
  • Reduction in asthma and eczema
